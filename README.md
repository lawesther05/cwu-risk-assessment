# Cybersecurity Risk Assessment: Critical Infrastructure

## Overview
This repository contains a comprehensive cybersecurity risk assessment conducted on Central Washington University's critical infrastructure. The report evaluates administrative, technical, and physical vulnerabilities and proposes actionable mitigation strategies to secure high-priority systems against rising cyber threats.

## Methodologies & Frameworks Used
* **Data Gathering:** RIIOT Methodology (Review, Interview, Inspect, Observe, Test)
* **Risk Analysis:** OCTAVE Allegro (Relative Risk Scoring & Heat Mapping)
* **Security Controls:** NIST SP 800-53

## Critical Systems Analyzed
The assessment evaluates the risk priority and dependencies of the following core systems:
* Student Information System (SIS)
* Finance and Payroll
* Identity and Access Management (IAM)
* Network Infrastructure

## Key Highlights
* **Threat Modeling:** Analyzed the potential impact of decentralized IT management, social engineering, malware, and unauthorized physical access.
* **Risk Visualization:** Calculated relative risk scores across multiple vectors and developed a Risk Management Heat Map to prioritize high-risk, high-probability threats.
* **Mitigation Strategy:** Recommended specific NIST controls (e.g., AT-2 for security awareness training, SI-3 for malware protection, PE-2 for physical access limits) to mitigate identified risks.
